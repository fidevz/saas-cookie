## Email Sequences

### 1. Welcome Sequence (triggers: new free signup)
Goal: Get them to their first success moment ASAP

| Email | Delay | Subject | Goal |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| #1 | Immediate | [Welcome — set expectations] | Open the relationship |
| #2 | Day 1 | [First thing they should do] | Drive first action |
| #3 | Day 3 | [Key feature spotlight] | Expand usage |
| #4 | Day 5 | [Social proof / case study] | Build confidence |
| #5 | Day 7 | [Soft upgrade mention] | Plant the seed |

---

### 2. Trial Nurture Sequence (triggers: trial started, not yet converted)
Goal: Convert before trial expires

| Email | Delay from trial start | Subject | Goal |
|-------|----------------------|---------|------|
| #1 | Day 1 | [You're in — here's how to win] | Activation |
| #2 | Day 3 | [Are you getting value yet?] | Check-in + help |
| #3 | Day 5 | [What [customer type] does in week 1] | Inspiration |
| #4 | Day 8 | [Your trial ends in [X] days] | Urgency |
| #5 | Day 10 | [Last chance — keep everything you've built] | Final push |
| #6 | Day 14 | [Trial ended — here's what you're missing] | Win-back |

---

### 3. Onboarding Sequence (triggers: became paying customer)
Goal: Drive adoption, reduce early churn

| Email | Delay | Subject | Goal |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| #1 | Immediate | [You're in — welcome to [product]] | Celebrate + next step |
| #2 | Day 2 | [The fastest way to [core outcome]] | Drive key action |
| #3 | Day 7 | [How to [advanced feature]] | Expand usage |
| #4 | Day 14 | [Check-in — how's it going?] | Catch issues early |
| #5 | Day 30 | [You've been with us a month 🎉] | Milestone + upsell |

---

### 4. Win-Back Sequence (triggers: customer churned or cancelled)
Goal: Re-acquire churned customers

| Email | Delay from churn | Subject | Goal |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------|
| #1 | Day 3 | [We're sorry to see you go] | Understand reason |
| #2 | Day 14 | [Here's what's new since you left] | Show improvement |
| #3 | Day 30 | [Special offer to come back] | Incentive |

---

### 5. Re-engagement Sequence (triggers: no email open in 60+ days)
Goal: Re-engage or clean the list

| Email | Delay | Subject | Goal |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| #1 | Day 0 | [Are you still there?] | Get a reaction |
| #2 | Day 7 | [Last chance — we'll miss you] | Final attempt |
| Action | If no open after #2 | — | Unsubscribe from list |

## Email Writing Rules

- **Subject line:** Max 50 chars, avoid spam words, test curiosity vs. direct
- **Preview text:** Complement the subject, never repeat it
- **From name:** [Founder name] or [Product name] — be consistent
- **Opening:** Never start with "I" — start with "You" or a statement
- **Body:** Short paragraphs (2–3 lines max), single column, mobile-first
- **CTA:** One primary CTA per email — button or bold link
- **PS:** Use P.S. for the second most important thing (often most read)

---

## Segmentation Rules

| Segment | Definition | Different treatment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Free users | Signed up, not paying | Focus on conversion |
| Trial users | In active trial | Urgency + value |
| Paid customers | Active subscribers | Retention + upsell |
| Churned | Cancelled | Win-back |
| Cold leads | Signed up > 30 days, never logged in | Re-engagement |

## Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Minimum acceptable | Target |
|--------|--------------------|--------|
| Open rate | 25% | >40% |
| Click rate | 2% | >5% |
| Unsubscribe rate | — | <0.3% |
| Spam complaint rate | — | <0.08% |

If open rate < 20%: review subject lines, test new angles
If unsubscribe rate > 0.5%: review content relevance and frequency
